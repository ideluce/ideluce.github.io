Isabelle Deluce

  • How'd She Get Here?

Previously

Determined to become Nicole Kidman, Isabelle found her own agent at the age of 10. She skipped real school to attend fake school as Matthew Perry’s on-screen student and got torn to shreds by zombies in a cult horror film. Most agree that her magnum opus is this Ring Pops commercial.

By her late teens, Isabelle turned her back on the film industry. She grew wary of the roles she auditioned for, which were either ‘ambiguously ethnic side-kick’ or 'inhumanely hot CW girl'. She tried her hand at academia, pursuing a Master’s in Information Systems & Design. Upon graduation, she was hired as a business consultant and UX Designer at Ernst & Young where she rubbed shoulders with Bay Street big wigs and drank $15 cold pressed juice.

Expensive juice couldn’t fill the void that film left behind. In 2018 Isabelle co-founded Peach Boy: a Toronto-based media production company. Today, with Peach Boy, Isabelle produces stories about characters she would have loved to audition for. Stories that not only give space for underrepresented voices, but actively amplifies them.

Writing & Directing

Isabelle writes, directs, and produces both narrative and branded content. Her work focuses on connecting people through vulnerability, humour, and sincerity.

She is currently working on Lina - a feature film she wrote and directed in collaboration with Peach Boy. Lina is about a young woman who navigates her unexpected gain of clout when she comes out of the closet. Check out the teaser, here.
