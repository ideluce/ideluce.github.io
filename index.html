Isabelle Deluce

Creative Producer, Retired Child Actor.

  • How'd She Get Here?

Previously

Determined to become Nicole Kidman, Isabelle found her own agent at the age of 10. She skipped real school to attend fake school as Matthew Perry’s on-screen student and got torn to shreds by zombies in a cult horror film. Most agree that her magnum opus is this Ring Pops commercial.

By her late teens, Isabelle turned her back on the film industry. She grew wary of the roles she auditioned for, which were either ‘ambiguously ethnic side-kick’ or 'inhumanely hot CW girl'. She tried her hand at academia, pursuing a Master’s in Information Systems & Design. Upon graduation, she was hired as a business consultant and UX Designer at Ernst & Young where she rubbed shoulders with Bay Street big wigs and drank $15 cold pressed juice.

Expensive juice couldn’t fill the void that film left behind. In 2018, Isabelle co-founded Peach Boy: a Canadian media production company. As an independent filmmaker, Isabelle produces stories about characters she would have loved to audition for. Stories that not only give space for underrepresented voices, but actively amplifies them.

Creative Producing

Got a creative idea? Isabelle will get it made.

As a Producer, Isabelle gives voice to storytellers from all walks of life, with a particular focus on LGBTQ+, BIPOC, and femme-identifying folks.

Her process is collaborative, empathetic, and structured to best fit the project's needs. Isabelle has worked with multidiscplinary artists to create music videos, short films, commercials, websites, and community-based arts events.

Isabelle provides artists the freedom to express their ideas, and the structure to realize them into tangible outputs.

Writing and Directing

Isabelle writes and directs both narrative and branded content. As a queer, bi-racial person of colour, her work - much like her life - is informed by complexity and nuance. Isabelle is interested in stories that explore universal truths through humour, sincerity, and vulnerability.
